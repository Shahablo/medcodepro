Problem

Coding breaks down where complexity goes up.

Surgeons, practice leaders, and billers all feel the cost of a workflow that is still too reactive, too manual, and too dependent on reconstruction after the case is already over.

For physicians

Too much ambiguity

Complex cases can require careful code and modifier selection, yet most support happens outside the clinical workflow.

For billers

Cold-chart reconstruction

Billing teams often work from documentation that may be incomplete, inconsistent, or missing the details that matter most.

For practices

Revenue leakage and rework

Missed codes, missed modifiers, and repeated clarification loops slow the revenue cycle and create avoidable friction.

Current workflow pain points

  • ✓Memory and static references are still doing too much work.
  • ✓Critical supporting details may never make it into the note.
  • ✓Fear of overcoding makes some physicians undercode.
  • ✓Disconnected teams are forced to reconcile interpretation differences later.
Why it happens

The system expects precision, but the tooling is still blunt.

The people doing the surgery are not coding experts, and the people coding often are not in the room. That gap creates a predictable pattern: underdocumentation, undercoding, modifier misses, and unnecessary follow-up work.
